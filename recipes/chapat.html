Chapati

Description

Chapati is a beloved unleavened flatbread that holds a cherished place in the hearts and homes of many across East Africa, South Asia, and the Caribbean. Its simple yet versatile nature makes it a staple accompaniment to a wide array of dishes or a satisfying snack on its own. Made from a mixture of whole wheat flour, water, salt, and sometimes oil, chapati dough is kneaded to perfection until it reaches a smooth and elastic consistency. This dough is then divided into small balls, rolled out into thin circles, and cooked on a hot griddle or skillet until it puffs up and develops golden brown spots on both sides.

Beyond its humble ingredients and straightforward preparation, chapati embodies a rich cultural heritage and a sense of communal warmth. It often takes center stage during festive celebrations, family gatherings, and everyday meals, symbolizing hospitality, togetherness, and the simple joys of sharing food with loved ones. Whether served alongside hearty curries, stews, or grilled meats, or enjoyed with a dollop of butter or a sprinkle of sugar, chapati transcends geographical boundaries to become a cherished culinary tradition that brings people together across cultures and generations.

Ingredients

  • 2½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 3 - 5 tbsp oil
  • 1¼ cup warm water
  • 6-8 tsp ghee, butter, or oil
  • Extra flour for dusting
  • 6 tsp oil for roasting

Steps

  1. Add flour, salt, and sugar in a mixing bowl. Mix well.
  2. Add 3-4 tbsp oil and rub into the flour.
  3. Add the measured warm water and form a soft dough. Add any extra water required, tablespoon at a time as you don't want the dough too soft.
  4. The dough should not be sticky.
  5. Take a tbsp of oil and knead it into the dough.
  6. Cover the dough with a damp cloth, lid, or cling film. Allow the dough to rest for 30 minutes.
  7. Divide the dough into six parts. Roll each part into a ball.
  8. Cover the balls with a damp cloth. Allow them to rest for 10-15 minutes.
  9. Dust the worktop with some flour.
  10. Roll one dough ball out into a circle about 6-7 inches in diameter.
  11. Smear about 1 tsp solid ghee or butter all over the rolled dough.
  12. Sprinkle a bit of flour all over.
  13. Fold the rolled dough into a fan shape. Check out the video.
  14. Then roll it into a spiral shape.
  15. Allow the dough to rest for 10 minutes. In the meantime can roll and shape the remaining 5 dough balls.
  16. Dust the worktop with some flour.
  17. Take one of the spiral dough and roll it out flat into a circle of about 6-7 inches in diameter.
